- Personal transparency → Relational authenticity → Economic fairness → Political accountability → Spiritual integrity → Physical health
- **Virtuous cycle**: Each domain reinforces the others

**Solution:** Focus on **easiest domain first** (often Physical—hard to fake). Build momentum across domains.

## The Six-Fold Consistency Test

**How to check for cross-domain hypocrisy:**

1. **State your core principle** (e.g., "I value honesty")
2. **Test in Personal domain**: Am I honest with myself?
3. **Test in Relational domain**: Am I honest in relationships?
4. **Test in Economic domain**: Am I honest in financial dealings?
5. **Test in Political domain**: Do I support honest governance?
6. **Test in Spiritual domain**: Is my spiritual practice honest?
7. **Test in Physical domain**: Is my physical behavior honest?

**If answer is "no" in any domain:** Hypocrisy detected. Either:
- **Change behavior** in that domain, OR
- **Revise principle** to reflect actual values

**Example:**
- Principle: "I value environmental sustainability"
- Personal: I educate myself on environmental issues ✓
- Relational: I encourage family to reduce waste ✓
- Economic: I buy cheap products with high environmental cost ✗
- Political: I vote for environmental policies ✓
- Spiritual: I see nature as sacred ✓
- Physical: I drive a gas-guzzling vehicle ✗

**Result:** Economic and Physical domain hypocrisy detected. Either:
- Change purchasing and transportation behavior, OR
- Revise principle to "I value environmental sustainability within my budget constraints"
